PEN World Voices Festival: Global Literature in NYC

By S.C. Thomas  |  August 14, 2025  |  NY Spotlight Report

The PEN World Voices Festival returned to New York City this year with a fervor that transcends mere celebration of literature, instead weaving a rich tapestry of global narratives into the vibrant cultural fabric of the city. From August 1st to August 7th, the festival transformed New York into a dynamic literary forum where cultural exchange and dialogue stood at the forefront. In its 21st edition, the festival continued to champion freedom of expression and spotlighted the urgency of storytelling in a world increasingly divided by geopolitical tensions.

This year’s theme, "Bridging the Divide," was poignantly relevant, reflecting the festival's role as a crucible for diverse voices from around the globe. More than 150 writers, poets, and thinkers from over 30 countries convened to share stories that confront issues such as displacement, identity, and the remnants of colonialism. The festival's opening keynote at the New York Public Library set the tone with Kenyan author Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o, whose profound insights into linguistic decolonization ignited the audience's imagination and underscored the power of language as a tool for liberation.

Throughout the week, venues across the city, from the bustling streets of Manhattan to the vibrant enclaves of Brooklyn, hosted engaging panels, intimate readings, and spirited debates. In a particularly resonant session, "Borders and Beyond," authors from conflict-ridden regions discussed the intricacies of writing about home from afar. Syrian novelist Samar Yazbek and Colombian journalist Patricia Engel shared personal narratives that both challenged and enriched global perspectives on migration and belonging.

The festival also embraced multimedia storytelling, with events that transcended traditional literary formats. At the Museum of Modern Art, an innovative installation combined short films with live readings, allowing attendees to experience narratives through a multisensory lens. This integration of visual and literary arts highlighted the festival's commitment to exploring new avenues of storytelling, ensuring that literature remains a dynamic force in the digital age.

One of the standout features of the festival was its focus on underrepresented voices. The "Emerging Voices" panel celebrated new authors who are reshaping the literary landscape with works that reflect diverse experiences and cultures. Nigerian-American author Akwaeke Emezi captivated audiences with their discussion on crafting narratives that defy genre boundaries, while Chinese debut novelist Xiaolu Guo offered an evocative exploration of diasporic identity.

In addition to author-specific events, the festival facilitated workshops that provided aspiring writers with practical tools for navigating the publishing industry. Sessions on digital storytelling and self-publishing were particularly popular, reflecting the growing interest in alternative pathways to literary success. These workshops, led by industry veterans and successful self-published authors, provided attendees with actionable insights and strategies, reinforcing the festival’s mission to empower voices across the literary spectrum.

While the festival primarily focused on literature, it did not shy away from engaging with broader socio-political issues. The panel "Literature as Resistance" delved into the responsibilities of writers in times of political upheaval, with speakers like Russian dissident poet Maria Stepanova and Turkish novelist Elif Shafak emphasizing the moral imperative to bear witness and challenge the status quo through the written word.

As the festival drew to a close, attendees were left with a sense of renewed purpose and inspiration. The PEN World Voices Festival successfully fostered a space where dialogue and diversity flourished, reminding participants of the unparalleled power of literature to bridge divides and foster empathy. In a world where cultural polarization often dominates headlines, the festival stood as a testament to the enduring influence of stories that transcend borders and time.

This year’s edition of the PEN World Voices Festival proved once again that New York City remains an unrivaled hub for global literary discourse, where the exchange of ideas and the celebration of diverse voices continue to shape the future of literature. As the city reverberates with the echoes of stories shared and connections forged, it becomes clear that in the realm of global literature, the PEN World Voices Festival is not just an event, but a movement.
