Call Down the Hawk: A Review of the Dreamer Trilogy Opener

By S.C. Thomas  |  December 05, 2023  |  NY Spotlight Report

Maggie Stiefvater's "Call Down the Hawk," the inaugural book in the much-anticipated Dreamer Trilogy, marks a triumphant return to the world she first brought to life in "The Raven Cycle." This new novel serves as both a sequel and a standalone, offering a rich tapestry woven from threads of magic, dreams, and darkness. Stiefvater's signature lyrical prose invites readers into a realm where dreams are not merely figments of imagination but potent forces that can reshape reality, for better or worse.

"Call Down the Hawk" focuses on Ronan Lynch, a character pivotal to the intricate narrative of "The Raven Cycle." Ronan is a dreamer, gifted—or cursed—with the ability to pull objects, people, and even nightmares from his dreams into the real world. In this new series, Stiefvater expands the mythology surrounding dreamers, introducing a fresh cast of characters with their own complex motivations and histories.

The novel delves into the mechanics and consequences of dreaming, presenting a universe where dreamers must navigate a fine line between creation and destruction. Stiefvater explores the internal and external conflicts arising from this delicate balance, placing Ronan at the center of a looming threat that could have cataclysmic repercussions. As Ronan grapples with his abilities, he is joined by his brothers, Declan and Matthew, whose personal arcs add depth to the narrative, exploring themes of family, identity, and acceptance.

Stiefvater's writing is as enchanting as ever, with a poetic quality that captivates the reader. Her ability to craft vivid, atmospheric settings is unmatched, drawing readers into a world that is at once familiar and fantastical. The novel’s pace is deliberate, allowing for deep character development and a gradual unfolding of the intricate plot. This contemplative storytelling style might not cater to those looking for a fast-paced fantasy, but for readers who appreciate a more immersive, character-driven experience, "Call Down the Hawk" offers profound satisfaction.

A compelling aspect of the novel is its exploration of the dreamers' new nemesis: a secretive organization determined to eradicate them. This antagonist brings an element of suspense and urgency to the story, posing questions about the ethics of power and the lengths to which individuals will go to control or destroy it. The presence of this shadowy group adds a layer of tension, complicating Ronan's journey and forcing him to confront the darker side of his abilities.

Maggie Stiefvater also skillfully introduces new characters who share the stage with the beloved figures from "The Raven Cycle." Among them is Jordan Hennessy, an art forger with a precarious connection to the dreamer world, and Carmen Farooq-Lane, a hunter with a complicated past. Their narratives intertwine with Ronan’s, enriching the story and expanding its scope. Stiefvater's talent for crafting multidimensional characters is evident in how she navigates these new relationships, providing readers with a satisfying blend of old and new dynamics.

While "Call Down the Hawk" is firmly rooted in the fantasy genre, its themes of creativity, agency, and the power of dreams resonate with a broader audience. Stiefvater deftly balances the fantastical elements with emotional realism, tackling questions of identity and belonging that will likely echo with readers well beyond the final page. The novel’s exploration of the unconscious mind as both a source of power and peril offers a thought-provoking commentary on the human condition itself.

In conclusion, "Call Down the Hawk" is a masterful opening to the Dreamer Trilogy, showcasing Maggie Stiefvater’s unique voice and imaginative prowess. It invites readers to journey alongside Ronan Lynch as he navigates a world where dreams can be dangerous—and sometimes deadly. For fans of "The Raven Cycle," this novel is a welcome expansion of a beloved universe, while newcomers will find it an intriguing entry point into Stiefvater's richly detailed world. As the series progresses, readers can anticipate an unfolding story that promises to be as complex and enthralling as a dream itself.
