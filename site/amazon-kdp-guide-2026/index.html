Amazon KDP Guide: Publish and Earn Royalties in 2026

NY Spotlight Report  ·  Updated March 2026

KDP Is Still One of the Best Passive Income Vehicles

Amazon's Kindle Direct Publishing lets anyone publish ebooks and paperbacks with royalties up to 70%. Here's exactly how it works in 2026.

What Sells on KDP

Low-content books (journals, planners, activity books, puzzle books) earn steady royalties with minimal writing required. The interiors are templated, covers are designed in Canva, and Amazon handles printing and shipping for paperbacks. Ebooks with niche expertise (technical guides, how-to manuals, local interest) perform well long-term.

Royalty Structure

Kindle ebooks: 70% royalty on books priced $2.99-$9.99. 35% outside that range. Paperback: 60% minus printing cost. A $7.99 paperback with $2.50 printing cost nets roughly $2.25 per sale.

The Low-Content Opportunity

Puzzle books, journals, and planners cost nothing to produce with AI tools and templates. A single puzzle book can earn $20-100/month passively. A catalog of 20-50 titles creates a meaningful royalty income floor with zero ongoing work.

Getting Started

Go to kdp.amazon.com, create an account, and click "+ Paperback" to start your first title. No setup fee. No listing fee. You only pay when a book sells (Amazon deducts printing costs from royalties).

Bottom Line

KDP is legitimately one of the best $0-investment passive income vehicles. Start with one low-content book, learn the system, then scale to 10-50 titles for a reliable royalty income floor.
